Kat’s Hot Take: What’s Shaping the Market Right Now

Big market shifts are creating new opportunities for buyers, sellers, and investors. Here’s what you need to know!

Bank of Canada Cuts Rates – What It Means for You

On January 29, 2025, the Bank of Canada lowered its key interest rate to 3%, making borrowing more affordable. This marks a major shift from the high rates of 2023-24, when mortgage costs were significantly higher.

New Interest Rates:

Overnight rate (affects mortgage rates): 3%

Bank Rate (what banks pay to borrow money): 3.25%

Deposit Rate (what banks earn on deposits): 2.95%

Even more interesting? The Bank is ending quantitative tightening (measures to slow inflation) and will restart asset purchases in March. If inflation remains under control, we could see even lower borrowing costs later this year.

How This Affects You:

Buyers: Lower mortgage rates make homeownership more affordable—time to get pre-approved!

Sellers: More buyers in the market = faster sales and stronger offers.

Investors: Cheaper financing makes rental properties a smart move.

📌 Next rate announcement: March 5, 2025—another cut could be on the horizon!

📌 Source: Bank of Canada Press Release (Jan 29, 2025)

Leadership Change & Real Estate Outlook

With Prime Minister Justin Trudeau stepping down, Canada is entering a leadership transition ahead of the next federal election on October 20, 2025. The Liberal Party will select a new leader, and as the election approaches, housing policy is expected to be a key issue.

💡 How could this impact the housing market?

Government transitions often bring new housing policies, tax changes, or incentives—stay tuned!

Some buyers and sellers may wait to see how policies unfold, but dropping interest rates could keep the market active.

Expect discussions on homeownership support, rental policies, and housing supply solutions leading up to the election.

📌 Source: Radio-Canada Analysis on Trudeau’s Resignation & Election Timing

U.S. Tariffs & Housing Costs – What’s the Real Impact?

While no new U.S. tariffs were introduced in 2025, existing tariffs on softwood lumber remain, keeping construction costs high. This could put pressure on new home prices and make resale homes more attractive to buyers.

💡 Key Takeaways:

Builders are still paying higher prices for materials, leading to higher costs for new builds.

Resale homes may see higher demand as buyers look for alternatives to pricey new builds.

If trade agreements change, we could see some relief, but for now, the market is holding steady.

📌 Source: Bank of Canada’s Monetary Policy Report (Jan 2025)

Ottawa Spotlight: Celebrating Black History Month

Black History Month, February 2025

February is a time to honour and celebrate the achievements, history, and contributions of Black Canadians.

💡 Did you know?

Ottawa is home to one of Canada’s most historic Black communities—Africville. Though originally in Halifax, the legacy of Black settlers in Ottawa dates back to the 19th century, contributing to the city’s rich cultural fabric.

🌟 Ways to celebrate in Ottawa:

Visit the Canadian Museum of History to explore exhibits on Black Canadian pioneers.

Support local Black-owned businesses and restaurants.

Read books by Black Canadian authors like Lawrence Hill and Esi Edugyan.

Learn more: February is Black History Month
